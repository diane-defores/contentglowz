# Branding Guide (Website)

## Brand

- **Brand name**: ContentFlow
- **Category**: AI-assisted content operations website and marketing.
- **Primary message**: "AI-assisted, human-led content execution"
- **Primary promise**: Reduce cognitive overhead so teams publish with intention.

## Brand Positioning

The website presents ContentFlow as an execution assistant, not a fully automated ghost writer.
It should support trust, clarity, and transparency before conversion intent.
All public claims must stay aligned with `contentflow_app`, which is the canonical product/business truth.

## Voice and Tone

- Clear, practical, and direct.
- Professional but not corporate.
- Concise with explicit, concrete outcomes.
- Avoid overpromising autonomy; emphasize human-in-the-loop control.

## Language

- Primary language on website copy is English.
- **When writing in French, use informal address ("tu") consistently (never "vous").**
- **When writing in French, always use proper French accents.**
- Use short, explicit labels and clear call-to-action language.

## Visual and UX Language

- Functional-first layout with strong affordances and readable hierarchy.
- Microcopy in status, disclaimers, and trust signals should be plain and precise.
- Accessibility and clarity are part of brand quality, not only implementation details.

## Terms to Use

- "human review"
- "content workflow"
- "publish with intention"
- "degraded mode"
- "trust and continuity"

## Terms to Avoid

- "miracle", "fully automatic", "set and forget"
- exaggerated claims implying no human control
- vague promises around guaranteed outcomes

## Experience Principles

- Keep users in control, even when systems are degraded.
- Ensure next steps are obvious in 1 sentence.
- Preserve momentum with explicit recovery and retry paths.
- Make uncertainty explicit and actionable.
