# SEO Deployment

Turn your content ideas into published, SEO-optimized articles without the manual grind. Enter a topic, click Run, and let the AI handle research, writing, optimization, and publishing.

---

## Why Use SEO Deployment?

### The Problem

Creating SEO content the traditional way takes hours per article:
- **2+ hours** researching competitors and keywords
- **3+ hours** writing and structuring content
- **1+ hour** optimizing for search engines
- **30+ minutes** formatting and publishing

That's 6+ hours per article. For a content team publishing weekly, that's over 300 hours per year on repetitive tasks.

### The Solution

SEO Deployment automates the entire pipeline. You provide the topic, and the system delivers a fully optimized, published article.

| Traditional Approach | With SEO Deployment |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| Research competitors manually | Automatic competitive analysis |
| Write content from scratch | AI-generated, human-quality content |
| Manually add schema markup | Technical SEO applied automatically |
| Copy-paste into your CMS | Direct publish to your repository |
| **6+ hours per article** | **Under 10 minutes** |

## The Pipeline: What Happens Behind the Scenes

When you click Run, six specialized AI agents work together:

### 1. Research

The system analyzes your competition:
- What are the top 10 ranking articles for this topic?
- What keywords are they targeting?
- What questions are searchers asking?
- What gaps exist in current content?

### 2. Strategy

Based on research, the system plans your content:
- Optimal content angle to differentiate from competitors
- Target keywords (primary and secondary)
- Recommended word count and structure
- Internal linking opportunities

### 3. Content Creation

AI writes your article with:
- Engaging introduction that hooks readers
- Well-structured sections with clear headings
- Natural keyword integration (no stuffing)
- Actionable takeaways and conclusions

### 4. Technical SEO

Automatic optimization including:
- Meta title and description
- Schema.org structured data
- Header hierarchy (H1, H2, H3)
- Image alt text suggestions
- Internal link recommendations

### 5. Editorial Review

Quality control before publishing:
- Consistency check across sections
- Tone and voice alignment
- Fact verification flags
- Readability scoring

### 6. Publishing

Direct to your repository:
- Formatted for your static site generator
- Proper frontmatter and metadata
- Committed and pushed automatically
- Ready for your CI/CD to deploy

## Best Practices

### Start with Single Runs

Before running large batches, test with a few single topics:
- Verify the output matches your brand voice
- Check that technical SEO meets your standards
- Confirm publishing works with your repository

### Use Dry Run for Important Topics

For cornerstone content or sensitive topics:
- Enable Dry Run to preview without publishing
- Review the generated content
- Make adjustments if needed
- Re-run with publishing enabled

### Build Topic Clusters with Batch

Maximize topical authority:
1. Create your pillar page manually (or via Single Run)
2. Batch 5-10 supporting cluster topics
3. The system automatically links them together
4. Your topic cluster builds domain authority

### Schedule for Consistency

Search engines reward consistent publishing:
- Set a realistic schedule you can maintain
- Start with weekly, scale to more frequent
- Monitor quality and adjust topics as needed

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does one article take?

Most topics complete in 5-10 minutes, depending on research depth and content length.

### Can I edit the content after it's published?

Yes. Content is pushed to your repository as markdown files. Edit them like any other content.

### What if I don't like the output?

Use Dry Run mode to preview before publishing. You can also edit after publishing or re-run with adjusted parameters.

### Does it work with my static site generator?

Yes. The platform supports Astro, Next.js, Gatsby, Nuxt, Hugo, and Jekyll. Content is formatted for your specific framework.

### Can I run multiple batches at once?

One deployment runs at a time to ensure quality. Queue up batches or use scheduling for continuous content.

### How do I stop a running deployment?

Click the Stop button. The current step will complete, then the deployment halts.
